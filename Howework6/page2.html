A little story

Out past Pluto, Mina’s tiny repair ship latched onto a dead satellite that shouldn’t have been there.

Its hull was warm.

Her scanner showed no power, no signal—just an impossible heartbeat of heat pulsing through metal that had drifted cold for a century.

She pressed her glove to the surface. The warmth pressed back.

A thin seam appeared, like an eyelid opening.

And in the quiet between stars, something inside the satellite whispered into her suit radio:

“Finally. A hand.”
